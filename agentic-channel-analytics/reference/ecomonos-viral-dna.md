## 1. The lexicon IS the product

The brand is not "economics with jokes." It is a **consistent private language**.
Every real-world concept has a fixed monkey translation, reused across videos. This
glossary is the **defensible moat** — copycats who skip the coherent language don't land.

## 2. The pidgin voice (caveman grammar)

Systematic article-dropping and simplified syntax: *"Precio sube"*, *"Mono tiene
miedo"*, *"Mono espectador sabe"*. This is the **sonic signature**. It breaks the
"serious finance video" register from the first second and builds complicity.

- Drop most articles ("el/la/los") and many connectors.
- Third-person "Mono" as subject for the viewer and actors.
- Short declaratives. One idea per sentence (also a production requirement).

## 3. Hook + retention architecture

- **Cold open, in-scene, zero intro.** *"Mono San Platman se subió a rama más alta…"* /
  *"Existe acuerdo secreto que lleva 50 años…"*. Never "hola, hoy os hablo de".
- **Counterintuitive thesis or secret** in title + first 5s: *"quizás esto es una
  burbuja"*, *"el truco más grande de la historia"*, *"nadie te lo explica"*.
- **Stacked similes** every few beats: *"escalera de tontos"*, *"GPS que nunca
  actualiza"*, *"gelatina con TDH"*, *"como cuando la mona que te gusta te deja en
  visto"*. Relatable absurd comparisons are the glue between concepts.
- **The "this affects YOU" pivot.** Abstract macro always lands in the viewer's
  pocket: *"aunque no inviertas en IA, pagarás tú la factura de luz, amigo mono."*
- **Chapters** in long videos (V3, V4) — promises and pays off, holds the curve.
- **Nuanced landing.** Ends with balance (*"algunas aportan valor real y otras
  ficticio"*, *"el sistema híbrido funciona mejor"*) → partially defuses the
  "misinformation" critique and signals rigor.

## 4. Loop engineering (the channel-growth engine)

- **Chained CTA to another video, always.** *"Ir a ver vídeo donde Mono explica por
  qué el sistema te condena a ser pobre."* → manufactures multi-video sessions.
- **Like-bait in-universe.** *"Tú das like y yo doy plátano."*
- **Sponsor inside the story.** Odoo = "Odu", "Moncho" running his business — no hard
  cut, monetizes without breaking immersion. Critical given they lost AdSense.

## 5. Emotional drivers (the motor)

Fear + generational grievance (boomers, "el sistema te condena") + revealed-secret /
conspiracy framing + us-vs-them + a final glimmer of hope. Same cocktail in all 5.

## 6. Pacing = production constraint

Very short sentences, one idea per beat — this is not just style, it maps to the
repo's pipeline (1 sentence = 1 image = 1 visual beat). Long videos still keep the
beat granularity; they just stack more beats and add chapter markers.

## The repeatable skeleton (per video)

1. **Cold open** in-scene with a named character/event + counterintuitive claim.
2. **Translate the news** into the lexicon (the "traducción de monolador" move).
3. **Build the mechanism** with stacked similes, one beat per sentence.
4. **Chapter escalation** (for long-form) toward a twist / hidden actor.
5. **Pivot to the viewer's pocket** ("esto te afecta a ti").
6. **Integrated sponsor** woven into a character's story (optional).
7. **Nuanced landing** to signal credibility.
8. **Chained CTA** to the next video + in-universe like-bait.
